User Research Discovering User Needs

Project Description

The purpose of this project was to dig into user pain points and ideate how travel experiences could be improved.

After conducting the interviews and checking survey results, it helped me get a better grasp on real people. It’s hard to make developed personas from your mind from scratch, but interviewing people gave me immediate insight into individuals’ travel experiences and how that related to their job, personality, habits, etc. It was interesting to see the correlations and also the discrepancies. This was very time-efficient and based on real data, which made it more valuable to me than creating personas from my mind.

There were a few common findings for user’s needs. People really enjoy unexpected (positive) surprises. These were examples of what people said “made their trip a good one.”

  • Hotel upgrades
  • Early check-in
  • Hotel happy hour/guest welcoming

These were some of the pain points:

  • Difficulty in communication, bad manners
  • Extra hidden booking fees
  • Unclear in details about the reservation

Conclusion

There are many great opportunities for design features, such as including information about the area to rate the safety, walkability, etc. so that users can have a solid idea of the type of area they will be staying in without having to do research for themselves using external sources (such as Google).

You can also have information about which hotels offer free room upgrades, and how frequently. (i.e. “Very often” “on occasion” “rarely” “upgrades not available”) Many hotels already frequently upgrade rooms so this is something that should be marketed – can only help them look better and increase user interest in staying with them.
